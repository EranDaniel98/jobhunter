[Day 5/9]

I'm preparing for an interview that doesn't exist yet.

No job posting. No application. No recruiter call.

Just a company I found yesterday through hiring signals - and a dossier that came back in about a minute.

Their tech stack. Who I'd report to. What questions they're likely to ask. Why they should care about my background.

The output that stopped me: a "Why hire me" paragraph I didn't write.

It connected my distributed systems experience to a scaling problem they mentioned in a blog post last month. I wouldn't have found that connection on my own.

That's the difference between showing up qualified and showing up inevitable.

It also flagged a gap. Their stack includes a tool I haven't used in production. Now I know to address that before anyone asks.

Not everything landed - the culture score pulled from stale Glassdoor data after a leadership change.

But here's what changed.

I used to walk into interviews hoping the right questions would come up. Now I walk in knowing which questions are coming - at a company that hasn't even posted the role.

Preparation without a job listing feels strange - like studying for an exam no one assigned. Until you realize no one else studied either.
