[Day 8/9]

Three things my AI job search system still gets wrong.

I've spent 7 days showing you what works.

Today: what doesn't.

1. Stale data lies with confidence.

My company dossier pulled Glassdoor reviews on a fintech I was targeting.

"Strong engineering culture. Collaborative leadership."

The CTO had left 4 months earlier. Half the senior team followed.

The AI doesn't know when data expired. It just trusts the source.

Fix: I cap leadership data at 14 days, culture reviews at 90. Better. Not solved.


2. One wrong word breaks trust.

The outreach agent called a lending startup a "payments platform."

Close. But wrong.

If I'd sent that, the founder would've known in 3 seconds I didn't actually read their site.

Fix: Every draft now goes through a category-check pass before it hits my inbox for approval. Caught it twice this week. Once on a healthtech I almost called a "wellness app."


3. Readiness score isn't actual fit.

Yesterday I showed you a 34 to 78 jump on a job posting analysis.

Here's the thing: that score measures presentation. How well my resume mirrors the posting.

It doesn't know if I'd actually thrive there. It can't read a room.

That gap is mine to close. Always will be.

The moat isn't the AI.

It's knowing exactly where the AI ends and you begin.

Most tools won't tell you that. I'd rather you know upfront.
