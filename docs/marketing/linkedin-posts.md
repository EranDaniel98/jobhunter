## Post [2] — The Method (Quick)

**Type:** Story
**Product Mention:** Subtle (last line)
**CTA:** Discussion

**Headline Options:**
- **A:** The job search method that worked — in 4 steps and 15 minutes per company
- **B:** 70-85% of jobs are never posted. Here's how to find them anyway.
- **C:** Stop applying. Start researching. Here's the exact playbook.

### English

The method a career strategist taught me, in 4 steps:

**Step 1: Find companies about to hire — before they post.**
A Series B announcement = expanding headcount. New VP of Engineering = team restructuring. Product launch = they need builders. These signals are on Crunchbase, LinkedIn, and company press pages. Most people never look.

**Step 2: Find the right person.**
Not HR. Not careers@. The hiring manager for your function, or the team lead who'd be your direct manager. LinkedIn + Hunter.io.

**Step 3: Research for 15 minutes.**
What's happening at the company. What they need. Why your background is relevant. You need one specific connection point.

**Step 4: Write a short, specific message.**
Reference the signal you found. Connect it to your experience. Ask for a 15-minute call. Five sentences max.

This method converts at 30-40% response rate vs 4-10% for job boards. The tradeoff: it takes 15-20 minutes per company.

That's the gap I'm automating. The system I'm building does steps 1-3 in about 90 seconds — and generates a first draft for step 4 that you review before sending.

But the method works by hand too. Try it this week on 3 companies and see what happens.

### עברית

השיטה שיועצת קריירה לימדה אותי, ב-4 צעדים:

**צעד 1: מצאו חברות שעומדות לגייס — לפני שהן מפרסמות.**
הודעה על סבב B = מגדילים צוות. VP Engineering חדש = רה-ארגון. השקת מוצר = צריכים בונים. הסיגנלים האלה נמצאים ב-Crunchbase, LinkedIn ועמודי עיתונות. רוב האנשים לא מסתכלים.

**צעד 2: מצאו את האדם הנכון.**
לא HR. לא careers@. מנהל הגיוס של הפונקציה שלכם, או ראש הצוות שיהיה המנהל הישיר. LinkedIn + Hunter.io.

**צעד 3: חקרו 15 דקות.**
מה קורה בחברה. מה הם צריכים. למה הרקע שלכם רלוונטי. אתם צריכים נקודת חיבור ספציפית אחת.

**צעד 4: כתבו הודעה קצרה וספציפית.**
ציינו את הסיגנל שמצאתם. חברו אותו לניסיון שלכם. בקשו שיחה של 15 דקות. חמישה משפטים מקסימום.

## Post [3] — Candidate DNA

**Type:** System
**Product Mention:** Moderate (showing the product)
**CTA:** Discussion

**Headline Options:**
- **A:** I uploaded my resume. The AI told me things about my career I hadn't seen myself.
- **B:** "Candidate DNA" — what happens when AI reads your resume deeper than any ATS
- **C:** Your resume is a document. Your Candidate DNA is a profile. Here's the difference.

### English

I uploaded my resume to the system I'm building. In about 30 seconds, it came back with something I didn't expect.

Not just a list of skills — I've seen that before. It built what I call a "Candidate DNA" profile:

An experience summary that captured my career arc, not just my last job title. Strengths I hadn't thought to articulate. Gaps I knew about but hadn't admitted. A career stage classification. And transferable skills I had from previous roles that I wasn't highlighting.

The system extracted over 60 skills — categorized as explicit (things I listed), transferable (things I demonstrated but didn't name), and adjacent (skills I'm close to having based on my trajectory).

Then it turned the whole profile into a vector — a 1536-dimensional mathematical representation of my professional identity. It uses this to match me against companies using semantic similarity, not keyword matching. So when a company's needs align with my actual experience, it surfaces the match — even if we're using different words for the same things.

No ATS does this. They match keywords. This matches meaning.

What would you want to know about your career profile that your resume doesn't tell you?

### עברית

העליתי את קורות החיים שלי למערכת שאני בונה. תוך 30 שניות, חזר לי משהו שלא ציפיתי לו.

לא רק רשימת כישורים — את זה ראיתי. היא בנתה מה שאני קורא פרופיל "DNA מועמד":

סיכום ניסיון שלכד את קשת הקריירה שלי, לא רק את התפקיד האחרון. חוזקות שלא חשבתי לנסח. פערים שידעתי עליהם אבל לא הודיתי בהם. סיווג שלב קריירה. וכישורים transferable מתפקידים קודמים שלא הדגשתי.

המערכת חילצה מעל 60 כישורים — מסווגים כ-explicit (דברים שרשמתי), transferable (דברים שהדגמתי אבל לא נקבתי בשם), ו-adjacent (כישורים שאני קרוב אליהם על בסיס המסלול שלי).

אחר כך היא הפכה את כל הפרופיל לוקטור — ייצוג מתמטי ב-1536 ממדים של הזהות המקצועית שלי. היא משתמשת בזה כדי להתאים אותי לחברות דרך דמיון סמנטי, לא התאמת מילות מפתח. ככה כשהצרכים של חברה מתיישרים עם הניסיון שלי בפועל, ההתאמה עולה — גם אם אנחנו משתמשים במילים שונות לאותם דברים.

## Post [4] — Finding Companies Before They Post

**Type:** System
**Product Mention:** Moderate

**Headline Options:**

- **B:** What happens when AI monitors funding rounds, leadership changes, and hiring velocity for you
- **C:** I told the system my target industry. It came back with companies I'd never heard of.

### English

I told the system: backend engineering, fintech, Tel Aviv area, mid-size companies.

It came back with 12 matches. Three of them had strong hiring signals — recent funding, new engineering leadership, and active job postings in adjacent roles. None of these three appeared on any job board for my specific function.

Here's what it actually does:

It monitors funding databases for recent rounds. Checks for leadership changes that signal restructuring. Tracks hiring velocity — when a company suddenly posts 5 backend roles, they're expanding the team even if your exact title isn't listed. Then it cross-references all of this against my Candidate DNA profile to produce a fit score.

Each company gets ranked. The ones with the highest combination of hiring signals + profile match surface first.

Under the hood, it's aggregating data from Hunter.io, NewsAPI, and job board APIs, then scoring signals against my career vector using cosine similarity in PostgreSQL. But what I see as a user is just: "these companies are probably hiring, here's why, and here's how well you match."

The manual version of this — scanning Crunchbase, LinkedIn, and news for 12 companies — would take me about 3 hours. The system did it while I made coffee.

What tools do you use to find companies that are hiring but haven't posted yet?
