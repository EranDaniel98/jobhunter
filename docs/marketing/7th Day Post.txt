[Day 7/9]

I found what looked like the perfect role.

Backend engineering. Fintech. Series B. My exact search criteria.

I pasted the posting. The system came back with a readiness score.

34 out of 100.

Then I read the breakdown.

The posting listed 14 requirements. I matched 9. But the system wasn't scoring what I had. It was scoring how I presented it.

My resume said "built microservices." The posting said "designed distributed event-driven architectures." Same work. Different framing.
The system flagged it as a gap because an ATS would too.

Three of my "missing" skills weren't missing at all. They were buried in bullet points that didn't use the right keywords.

The resume tips were specific. Not "add more metrics." Actual rewrites. 
"Move distributed systems experience to line 1 of your summary."
"Replace 'built APIs' with 'designed and scaled RESTful services handling X requests/day.'"

I made the changes. Ran the analysis again.

78.

A 44-point jump. Same person. Same experience. Different presentation.

The job posting wasn't the problem. My translation was.

This only works if the experience is real. The system doesn't invent skills.
It finds the ones you already have and puts them where an ATS will actually see them.

It doesn't make you more qualified. It makes you more visible.
